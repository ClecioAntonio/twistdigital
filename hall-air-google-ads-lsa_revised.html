The Platforms

Google Ads and Local Services Ads: how each platform works

Google offers two paid advertising systems for service businesses. They charge differently, appear in different positions, and reach customers at different stages of their decision.

Pay Per Click

🔎 Google Ads (Search)

Text ads at the top of Google when someone searches for HVAC or plumbing services. You pay each time a user clicks, regardless of whether they call or book.

  • Full control over keywords, ad copy, and budget
  • Target specific services: AC tune-up, leak repair, drain cleaning
  • Appear instantly no wait for organic ranking
  • Call extensions allow one-tap dialing from mobile
  • Detailed analytics: impressions, clicks, conversions
  • Seasonal budget adjustments (summer AC peak, winter heating)
Pay Per Lead

🛡️ Local Services Ads (LSA)

Verified listings above everything on Google above regular ads, above the map, above organic results. You pay only when a customer calls or messages you directly.

  • Absolute top of Google search results
  • Google Verified badge builds immediate trust
  • Pay per lead, not per click no spend on casual browsers
  • Customers call directly from the results page
  • Reviews and ratings displayed prominently
  • Dispute credits available for invalid leads

Campaign Strategy

How to structure the campaigns for Hall Air

The Richmond area HVAC and plumbing market peaks from late May through August for AC and in December through January for heating. Campaigns should be structured around these two cycles from the start.

01

Separate ad groups by service

One group for HVAC, one for plumbing. Each with its own keywords, ad copy, and landing page. Relevance lowers cost per click.

02

Prioritize high-intent keywords

"AC repair Richmond VA," "emergency plumber near me," "HVAC tune-up Richmond." These searches convert at 10 – 25%.

03

Add negative keywords

Exclude "DIY," "how to," "free," "parts only." Filters out non-buyers and reduces wasted spend from day one.

04

Enable call extensions

Show Hall Air's number directly in the ad. Most urgent searches happen on mobile and convert via call, not form.

05

Use location-specific copy

References to specific Richmond neighborhoods Midlothian, Short Pump, Henrico, Chesterfield in headlines and landing pages outperform generic city-level targeting. Homeowners respond to names they recognize.

06

Adjust bids seasonally

Richmond summers are hot and humid budget should increase 30 – 50% from May through August for HVAC. Winter plumbing emergencies (frozen pipes, water heater failures) tend to spike during cold snaps, which in Richmond typically hit in January.

🛡️

Getting Google Verified for LSA

Required: business license, liability insurance, and background checks for the owner and all field technicians. A verified Google Business Profile is also mandatory and must match the LSA account exactly — name, address, and phone number. The process takes 2 to 4 weeks. Insurance must stay current to remain active.

Recommended Starting Budget

To achieve 10 combined clicks per day across HVAC and plumbing through Google Ads Search, Hall Air should plan for $3,000 – $3,900/month in ad spend. High-urgency keywords can spike to $20 – $55 per click during peak season, but a well-structured campaign avoids relying on those terms exclusively. Adding LSA adds roughly $1,000 – $2,400/month and only charges when a customer initiates contact. A combined starting budget of $4,000 – $6,300/month is a realistic and defensible range for both platforms running simultaneously. Both campaigns require 30 – 60 days to reach full optimization.

Month 1 – 2

📊 Learning Phase

Google's algorithm optimizes toward conversions. Expect higher CPCs and lower efficiency early. This is normal do not pause or drastically change campaigns during this window.

  • Set up conversion tracking (calls + form fills)
  • Identify top-performing keywords after week 2
  • Pause low-quality search terms via negative keywords
  • LSA verification completing in parallel
Month 3+

📈 Optimized Performance

Once Google has enough data, Smart Bidding begins reducing cost per conversion. Campaigns can be refined and budgets adjusted based on actual booked job revenue.

  • Switch to Target CPA once 30+ conversions tracked
  • LSA reviews accumulating lower CPL over time
  • Scale budget during summer AC and winter heating peaks
  • Retarget visitors who did not convert on first visit
⚠️

Note on LSA Cost Variability

LSA cost per lead can fluctuate significantly week to week. The same campaign may generate leads at $35 one week and $120 the next, depending on local competition, seasonal demand, and Google's internal ranking signals. Monthly averages are reliable for budgeting, but short-term swings are normal and should not trigger premature campaign changes.

Operational Factors

What determines whether the ads actually convert

Ad spend generates the call or click. Conversion depends on how Hall Air handles what comes next.

!

Response time

The first company to call back wins the job. Leads contacted within 5 minutes convert at significantly higher rates than those reached after 30+ minutes.

!

Google review count and rating

LSA rankings weight reviews heavily. A profile with 100+ reviews at 4.8 stars pays less per lead and appears more often than a newer profile with 20 reviews.

!

Phone answering rate

A click or LSA lead that goes to voicemail is a paid opportunity lost. Campaigns perform significantly better when calls are answered live during business hours.

!

Landing page speed and clarity

Most service searches happen on mobile. A slow or confusing page drops conversion rates regardless of ad quality. One focused page per service is the baseline.

!

Close rate and job value

return on ad spend (ROAS) depends on how well inbound leads are converted into booked jobs. A technician who adds a maintenance plan on a repair call changes the math entirely.

!

Conversion tracking setup

Without call and form tracking connected to Google Ads, the algorithm cannot optimize toward real conversions. This is a prerequisite before enabling Smart Bidding.

📌

The compounding advantage

A business with strong reviews, fast response, and good phone handling will consistently outperform a competitor spending twice as much on ads. The campaigns amplify what is already working operationally they do not replace it. Addressing these factors before or alongside launch will determine whether the investment generates a 3x or a 6x return.

🚫

Negative keywords from day one

These terms waste budget and should be excluded immediately: DIY, how to fix, free, parts, manual, certification, training, jobs, hiring, salary, cheap, warranty claim, homewarranty. Drain cleaning in particular attracts a lot of DIY searchers tight match types and negatives are essential for that ad group.

📱

Mobile-first setup

Over 70% of urgent home service searches happen on mobile. Every ad should have a call extension with Hall Air's direct number, location extensions showing the Richmond area service coverage, and a landing page that loads in under 3 seconds on 4G. Calls from ads should be tracked as conversions from day one.
